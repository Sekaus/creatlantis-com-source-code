Private Policy of The CreAtlantis.com Web Platform

Data Handling and Your Privacy When Using the Site

  1. About Our Handling of User Data

    1. General information about us

      We, as a team and administrators of this website, know how to take care of our user's data and privacy, we will therefore NOT disclose to third parties or employees any sensitive information such as credit card information and personal address, email, or password associated with the user's account without additional reasons or responsibilities.

      As a team and administrators of this website, we do NOT use cookies or any other unnecessary data tracking without additional reasons or instructions. On this site, we only use things like sessions. Sessions are a type of temporary storage of data that is stored both on the user's device and on the website's server. In this case, it is most time used to save the user's login so that they still are logged in when they visit another website or when they in a short time close their device and come back after a short time. Sessions will be deleted after 10 minutes and up to 24 hours of inactivity and will be deleted the next time a user tries to log in on the same device.
      However, we will still strongly suggest that the user log out manually in the menu if they ganna be offline for a while.

      When the user or another user on the page shares data, such as text and images and other types of submissions, that data is sent from the user's device to the website's server and then forwarded to other data services, such as AWS S3 public data (shared between the users) and our user data store private data (NOT shared between the users).
      On this website, we can also occasionally collect data for statistical purposes. This does NOT include data like gender, age, geography, personal address, or other private information private data (NOT shared between the users). Our statics contains the history of our users' activity and describe the posts the users see and click on and which feedback they give, such as faves, stars, and comments public data (shared between the users).

      You can read more about the different data services we use on the website in the next chapter, 2.1 to 2.2.

  2. The Different Data Dervices on This Website

    1. Public Data

      Public data are shared between the users on the website and are accessible by anyone on both this website and outside this website (third-party).
      Public data is read-only and can only be edited or deleted by the owner and the admins if they want.
      User posts and blogs like journals and images are under the category of public data and are stored and shared on an Amazon S3 (Simple Storage Service) file server.
      NO sensitive data like personal addresses, email, or passwords are stored there.
      Read more about Amazon S3 (Simple Storage Service) and its data handling.

    2. Private Data

      Private data are NOT shared between the users on the website or outside this website (third-party). Private data is only used to identify ownership of accounts and content and is stored on a separate server that only the admins with specialist knowledge and permission can access. ONLY the owner of their account and user information and content can change sensitive data like personal addresses, email, or passwords.
      The admins of the servers and website will NEVER ask for passwords or credit card information.
      NEVER share your password with anyone, not even someone you know.

  3. Remember to Keep Yourself Updated

    Remember to keep yourself up to date on any changes to our Private Policy that may come after you have read this page. In many cases, if it has a huge enough impact on the website, we will inform the users by a popup, note, or email with the changes we make to our Private Policy. BUT be aware that we can at ANY time if needed, without notice to the user, change anything that has been written here on the page to make it better fit the uses of the side and the admins. As the admins of the website and its content, WE have full right to change and add other information and rules if needed, therefore it is IMPORTANT that you keep yourself updated and keep an eye on any changes that may come over time.
